Overview

LeasePlan is the world's leading fleet management and vehicle leasing company, operating across 30+ countries. In India, its digital presence had fragmented across four separate portals — each built on different frameworks, serving overlapping user groups, with no unified customer journey.

Engaged through HCL Technologies, I led the digital foundation strategy — conducting a full heuristic audit, building user personas, mapping task flows, and defining the architecture of a single unified platform across web and mobile.

The challenge was as much organisational as it was technical: siloed portals meant siloed teams, duplicated costs, and a customer experience that felt transactional rather than relational. The business was losing renewals it had already earned.

The Problem

Portal

Four portals, zero cohesion

Transactional, not relational

  • Portal felt like a utility, not a partner

Invisible to the right people

Key research findings
01
Mobile-first is non-negotiable

78.98% of Indian internet traffic arrives via mobile. Every LeasePlan portal was desktop-only, with zero responsive design — effectively locking out the majority of users.

02
Performance is costing conversions today

47% of users expect a page to load within 3 seconds. SalaryPlan's 60-second load time and MyLeasePlan's 69/100 performance score were actively destroying acquisition.

03
The business was flying blind

MyLeasePlan had zero Google Analytics implementation. No bounce data, no session data, no demographic insight — decisions were being made without any user evidence.

04
Automation delivers a 92% time reduction

The LP Hunter quote process currently takes 90 minutes through manual handoffs. Full digital automation reduces this to approximately 7 minutes — directly improving conversion.

05
Users had no reason to return

No onboarding, no loyalty loop, no engagement triggers. The portal was a service utility, not a relationship — and users were behaving accordingly, with low repeat visits and low renewal intent.

UX Audit

Navigation

Cluttered primary navigation

Low-value links like "About LeasePlan" occupied prime nav real estate. The logo wasn't a home link. No breadcrumbs — users couldn't navigate back.

Forms

Manual data entry overload

Service booking required users to fill every field manually despite the data existing in the system. A modal popup on the Car Service page disrupted the entire flow.

Accessibility

Zero ALT tags sitewide

No alt attributes on any images or form fields, locking out screen reader users entirely. Combined with browser compatibility bugs, significant audiences were excluded.

Dashboard

Profile data displaced key actions

The dashboard prioritised static profile information over the service actions users needed most. Request for Service was neither highlighted nor differentiated.

Mobile

No mobile version, at all

Despite 79% of the target audience being on mobile, no portal had any responsive design. Mobile was entirely broken — and no one had measured the impact.

Analytics

Decisions made without data

MyLeasePlan had no analytics implementation. No bounce rate, no session data, no traffic sources — the team was iterating completely blind.

Recommendations

User Experience
Build a single unified portal with full multichannel presence
Consolidate all four portals into one responsive platform on a modern framework (React or Angular). Support Desktop, Mobile Web, and native App from a single codebase — reducing infrastructure from 3 servers to 1 and dev overhead from 4 to 2 engineers.
Performance
Resolve 95 validation errors and optimise for sub-3s load
Fix all HTML validation errors, implement image optimisation, enforce coding standards, and establish performance budgets across the CI/CD pipeline. Eliminate the Chrome popup bug and page resolution issues.
Engagement
Shift from service provider to trusted partner
Build a personalised dashboard with vehicle, insurance, and service status at a glance. Add WhatsApp chatbot integration, push notifications, proactive insurance reminders, and a loyalty and referral program.
Analytics
Implement Google Analytics across all pages, plus ComScore
Full GA deployment enables data-driven iteration for the first time. Add ComScore for competitive benchmarking. Establish reporting cadences so every future UX decision is grounded in user evidence.
SEO
Expand from "lease" to the full intent spectrum
Current SEO captures only lease-intent traffic. Expanding to hire, rental, car loan, and car booking keywords targets the full market. A unified domain also consolidates link equity split across four sites.
Marketing
Replace scatter-gun spend with targeted multi-channel campaigns
CPL campaigns for measurable lead generation. Google retargeting and UAC for multi-channel reach. Direct partnerships with business portals (LinkedIn, Economic Times). Facebook video for user acquisition. All spend tracked against unified analytics.

Outcomes

92%

Reduction in lead-to-quote time

The LP Hunter scenario took 90 minutes end-to-end through manual handoffs, email chains, and account servicing team dependencies. Full digital automation of quote generation, data pre-fill, and real-time approvals collapses this to approximately 7 minutes — a direct, measurable impact on car sales and conversion rate, with no additional headcount required.

Infrastructure cost halved

One unified portal reduces servers from 3 to 1 and engineering overhead from 4 to 2 for ongoing maintenance — while dramatically improving capability and quality.

Mobile-first for 79% of users

A fully responsive platform with a native app directly addresses the largest user segment, who currently experience a completely broken interface across all portals.

Loyalty loop drives renewals

Personalised dashboards, referral programs, and proactive service reminders create reasons to stay — directly countering the zero renewal motivation revealed in research.

Data-driven iteration from day one

Full analytics implementation means every future UX decision can be validated. The business gains a competitive intelligence foundation it has never had before.

09

Reflections

What worked

  • The user quote as an anchor. Opening every stakeholder session with the verbatim user feedback made alignment faster — no one could argue with a real customer saying they were about to leave.
  • Side-by-side portal audit. Mapping all four portals simultaneously made the duplication immediately visible and inarguable — a single slide did more persuasion than a hundred words.
  • One ROI number. The 90-minute → 7-minute Hunter scenario gave the entire team a single compelling data point to rally around throughout the engagement.
  • Iterative persona development. Keeping personas clearly labelled as "Phase 1 drafts" reduced stakeholder defensiveness and invited collaboration rather than critique.

What I'd do differently

  • Usability testing earlier. Heuristic review found many issues, but task-based sessions with real users would have surfaced friction that analysis alone couldn't see.
  • Analytics as Week 1 infrastructure. Every week without GA is data lost forever. I'd make analytics implementation a prerequisite before any other design work begins.
  • Accessibility as a baseline, not a retrospective. The zero-ALT-tag situation should have triggered an immediate remediation sprint — not a recommendation in a future phase.
  • A north-star metric from day one. Defining a single shared success metric (e.g., portal return visit rate) earlier would have unified design decisions across teams throughout the project.
