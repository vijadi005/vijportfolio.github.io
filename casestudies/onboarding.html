Overview

Context

Why this journey mattered

Advisor onboarding is a foundational experience. It shapes how new advisors perceive the brand, how quickly they become productive, and how effectively they can start doing business. The journey map showed that onboarding was not a single step, but a chain of interconnected stages involving recruitment, contracting, licensing, onboarding support, training, digital access, and continuous growth.

Business need: help advisors become activated faster and more successfully within the Manulife ecosystem.
User need: understand what to do, when to do it, and which tools, contacts, and systems support each stage.
Design value: expose the full frontstage and backstage service journey rather than treating onboarding as only a digital portal problem.
Journey structure

Four major stages

The map organizes the experience into four connected stages: awareness and recruitment, contracting and licensing, onboarding and training, and continuous growth and specialization. Across these stages, advisors interact with firm actions, Manulife actions, digital touchpoints, and internal systems that influence momentum, clarity, and ease of progress.

Frontstage: advisor actions, firm actions, and digital touchpoints visible to the advisor.
Backstage: Manulife internal actions, supporting systems, teams, and operations.
Insight layer: pain points reveal where the journey breaks down and where design can create value.
02

Problem framing

How might Manulife create an easier, clearer, and more connected onboarding journey so advisors can move from recruitment to readiness with less friction and greater confidence?
Advisor goals

What advisors were trying to achieve

Evaluate fit: understand the company, systems, and value proposition before committing.
Complete licensing and contracting: successfully submit applications, documentation, and required setup steps.
Gain readiness: access knowledge, tools, systems, and training needed to start working efficiently.
Build momentum: establish a sustainable practice with ongoing support, growth opportunities, and specialization.
Key pain points

Where the journey breaks down

Too many systems: advisors encounter multiple portals, tools, and disconnected touchpoints across stages.
Manual work and delays: applications, approvals, and setup tasks rely on handoffs that slow progress.
Limited visibility: it is not always clear what stage the advisor is in, what is missing, or who owns the next step.
Inconsistent support: training, communications, and follow-through vary by stage and by stakeholder involvement.
Fragmented onboarding experience: the journey feels operationally necessary, but not intentionally designed end to end.

04

Design principles

Principle 01
Make the path visible
Advisors should clearly understand where they are in the onboarding journey, what is required next, and what is blocking progress.
Principle 02
Reduce system fragmentation
Consolidate or better connect portals, tools, and communications so the journey feels coherent rather than scattered.
Principle 03
Support guided completion
High-friction tasks like contracting, licensing, and setup should be broken into guided, trackable steps with contextual support.
Principle 04
Design for readiness, not just access
Giving access to a portal is not enough; advisors need knowledge, training, and confidence to become productive.
Principle 05
Connect internal and external actions
Backstage handoffs, approvals, and support processes must align with frontstage expectations to prevent service breakdowns.
Principle 06
Extend onboarding into growth
The journey should continue beyond activation, helping advisors specialize, deepen engagement, and grow their practice over time.
05

Opportunity areas

01
Unified onboarding hub
Create a clearer single entry point that brings together recruitment status, contracting, licensing progress, onboarding tasks, and learning resources.
02
Stage-based progress tracking
Introduce progress visibility so advisors know what is complete, what is pending, and which internal or firm-side actions are next.
03
Guided contracting and licensing
Simplify document-heavy steps with clearer instructions, status indicators, reminders, and error prevention.
04
Integrated digital touchpoints
Better connect advisor portal, CE center, email, web app, and internal tools so the experience feels more continuous.
05
Role-based training experience
Support readiness with contextual onboarding content, task-based training, and easier access to required learning.
06
Proactive communication model
Reduce uncertainty with milestone alerts, reminders, confirmations, and timely outreach during each stage of the journey.
07
Clearer ownership and handoffs
Define who owns each step across advisor, firm, and Manulife teams to reduce ambiguity and service delays.
08
Continuous growth support
Extend the experience beyond activation with specialization pathways, growth guidance, and ongoing business enablement.
09
Feedback-informed improvement loop
Capture advisor and internal team feedback to continuously refine onboarding, support, and digital experience design.
06

Expected outcomes

Advisor value

A smoother path to readiness

More clarity around stages, requirements, and next actions.
Less friction across contracting, licensing, portal access, and training.
More confidence from better communication, guidance, and support.
Faster activation so advisors can start working effectively sooner.
Business value

A more scalable onboarding service

Improved efficiency through clearer handoffs, fewer delays, and better alignment between teams.
Higher advisor satisfaction from a more intentional and connected experience.
Stronger adoption of digital systems when tools feel easier to use and better integrated.
Better long-term growth by connecting onboarding with continued development and specialization.
The biggest shift was seeing onboarding not as a checklist, but as a service journey — one that connects recruitment, activation, learning, and long-term advisor growth.
